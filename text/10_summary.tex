\subsection{Executive Summary}

Sentry Management Solutions is a technology and system management partner, not a bus operator. We do not own school buses, employ CDL school bus drivers, or compete with the transportation companies that serve Rhode Island's students. Instead, we bring specialized capabilities in routing optimization, real-time dispatch, GPS tracking, fleet management, analytics, and video violation detection that complement bus operators and give RIDE independent, data-driven oversight of the entire Statewide Transportation System. Our MTA Access-A-Ride contract---a \$181 million program managing 10,000+ daily paratransit trips, 15,000 tracked vehicles, and 150+ transportation providers across New York City---demonstrates that a technology platform provider can operate at scale, across multiple operators, without the conflict of interest that arises when a bus company controls its own performance data.

Sentry's platform directly addresses the goals RIDE has articulated in this RFI: reviewing zone configurations for optimal bidding, fostering genuine partnership between the state and its vendors, and deploying resources efficiently across a system that has grown 34\% in ridership over the past decade. The platform enables RIDE to model alternative zone structures, quantify the cost and service impact of policy changes before implementing them, automate the ``expenditure follows the child'' billing process that currently requires 700 manual checks per year, and identify the 550 ghost riders and extreme deadhead inefficiencies that the Legislative Commission documented---all from a single, unified dashboard that RIDE controls.

The 2025 legislative session created immediate technology requirements that reinforce the need for a dedicated system management platform. The Live Digital Video Violation Detection Systems act mandates camera systems on all new buses by July 1, 2027---the very first day of the next contract. The expanded van definition (10 passengers) requires routing optimization that can identify van-eligible routes and model fleet conversion scenarios. The temporary driver license provision requires credential tracking systems that manage RI-specific and border-state licensing pathways simultaneously. These are technology challenges, not bus operation challenges, and they are best addressed by a technology specialist.

Sentry respectfully recommends that RIDE consider procuring the technology and system management layer as a separate contract from bus operations. This approach would increase competition by lowering barriers to entry for smaller bus operators who lack enterprise-grade technology, reduce vendor lock-in by ensuring that RIDE owns its routing and performance data independently of any single operator, and give RIDE direct, unfiltered oversight of operator performance across all zones. RIDE's existing System Manager model (Transpar) validates the concept of centralized management; Sentry proposes to upgrade the technology powering that structure with a modern, real-time, AI-enabled platform proven at a scale far larger than Rhode Island's 334 routes.

\subsection{Key Differentiators}

\begin{description}[style=nextline, leftmargin=0.5cm]
    \item[\faIcon{check-circle} \textbf{Scale Without Conflict:}] Sentry manages the technology platform for over 10,000 daily paratransit trips in New York City, coordinating 150+ transportation providers and tracking 15,000 vehicles in real time. We bring that proven scale to Rhode Island without the conflict of interest that arises when a bus operator controls its own technology---and therefore controls what performance data RIDE sees. When Sentry reports on-time rates, deadhead miles, or maintenance compliance, the data comes from an independent source with no incentive to minimize service failures or inflate performance metrics.

    \item[\faIcon{check-circle} \textbf{Video Violation Detection Readiness:}] Sentry is prepared to deploy live digital video violation detection systems statewide, meeting the July 1, 2027 mandate for all new buses and establishing the infrastructure for full-fleet compliance by 2032. The Providence pilot (7,000+ violations detected, approximately \$300,000 in FY2025 revenue from a limited deployment) demonstrates both the safety imperative and the revenue model. Scaling this capability across all 334 routes could generate \$1.5--2.5 million in annual revenue---with 30\% flowing to municipalities, 30\% to the technology vendor, and 40\% to the state---while protecting students at every bus stop in Rhode Island.

    \item[\faIcon{check-circle} \textbf{Vendor-Neutral Architecture:}] Sentry's platform is architecturally vendor-neutral, designed to work across multiple operators, vehicle types (Type A, B, C, D buses and 10-passenger vans), and zone configurations simultaneously. Whether RIDE awards zones to two operators or seven, maintains the current zone map or restructures to nine zones, Sentry provides a single pane of glass for statewide oversight. No bus operator's proprietary system can deliver this---because no operator has an incentive to provide RIDE with transparent, comparative performance data across its competitors.

    \item[\faIcon{check-circle} \textbf{Data-Driven Cost Reduction:}] Sentry's analytics platform directly targets the specific savings opportunities identified by the 2024 Legislative Commission and confirmed in RIDE's October 2024 system overview: ghost rider elimination (550 phantom riders generating phantom demand and unnecessary routes), deadhead reduction (36--51\% of total route time in Zones C and D is empty-bus travel), van conversion optimization (147 of 334 vehicles are already vans---the platform identifies additional routes eligible for conversion under the new 10-passenger law, at \$26,000 savings per vehicle replaced), and automated billing (replacing the 700 manual checks processed annually across 49 LEAs with GPS-verified, per-student cost allocation). These are not theoretical savings---they are quantified inefficiencies waiting for the analytical tools to address them.

    \item[\faIcon{check-circle} \textbf{Building on What Works:}] RIDE's existing System Manager model (Transpar), Parent GPS App (launched October 2024), and proactive outreach programs requiring operators to use digital systems for earlier family sign-up all demonstrate a clear commitment to centralized management and technology-driven operations. Sentry is not proposing to start from scratch---we propose to upgrade the technology powering a structure that RIDE has already validated over 15 years of statewide transportation management. By deploying this upgraded platform in advance of operator transitions---ideally through a separate, earlier contract award---Sentry ensures that routing data, dispatch systems, GPS tracking, driver credential verification, and maintenance compliance infrastructure are fully operational before Day One of service. This is the single most effective risk mitigation RIDE can implement to prevent a repeat of the September 2024 disruption, when an operator began service without adequate systems, drivers, or oversight in place.
\end{description}
